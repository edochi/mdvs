# Prismatiq Lab — Research Journal

Welcome to the internal knowledge base of the **Prismatiq** research group. This journal collects everything we produce and reference in our day-to-day work: project documentation, experiment logs, meeting minutes, blog posts, team profiles, and technical reference material.

We started this journal in early 2031 when the group moved from scattered Google Docs to a flat folder of markdown files. It grew quickly. By mid-2031 we had three active research projects, a small blog for internal communication, and enough meeting notes to justify a folder structure. The current layout reflects roughly a year of organic growth — some corners are tidy, others are not.

## Conventions

Most files carry YAML frontmatter with at least a `title` and `status` field. Project notes additionally track `author`, `tags`, and experiment-specific metadata. Meeting notes record `attendees` and `date`. Blog posts use `draft` and `tags`. People profiles are simple — name, role, contact info.

Some files, particularly in `reference/`, have no frontmatter at all. They are plain markdown documents that predate our frontmatter conventions. We keep them as-is.

Not every field appears in every file. Some fields are project-specific (e.g., `sensor_type` only appears in Alpha experiment logs). Some fields changed type over time — early notes used integers for `priority`, later ones used strings. This is intentional and reflects real-world drift in informal documentation.
