# Photonics Europe 2032 — Recap

We did it. Live demo, real data, no crashes. Well, one near-crash — the MetaMat Controller overheated during setup because the conference room was 28°C, but REMO noticed the thermal warning before it became a problem.

## The Talk

"Closed-Loop Calibration of Programmable Metamaterial Biosensor Arrays" — 25 minutes plus Q&A. The room was full (maybe 120 people). The live demo got the biggest reaction: we showed the sensor array detecting a glucose solution in real-time, with the drift correction visibly stabilizing the readout as people watched.

## Reactions

- The ETH Zürich group (from last year's conversation) is now formally proposing a collaboration. They want to apply our drift correction to their own sensor platform.
- A startup from Amsterdam asked about licensing. Too early for that, but flattering.
- Three people asked if REMO was "part of the demo." He was standing next to the poster. He was holding the poster. He was the one who calibrated the sensor before the demo. He is part of the team.

## Takeaways

This was the first time we showed the full Alpha-Beta pipeline publicly. The response confirms we're on the right track. Now we need to publish the paper, finish the ERC application, and figure out what a "product" version of this looks like.
