# REMO Reviews: The Hamamatsu SPEC-200X

Sara suggested I write a review of the new spectrometer for the internal blog. I am uncertain of the expected tone for a "review," but I will provide an objective assessment.

## Build Quality

The SPEC-200X is well-constructed. The optical path alignment is factory-calibrated to within 0.01 nm, which is adequate. The cuvette holder mechanism operates smoothly, though I note that human operators occasionally fail to seat the cuvette fully, resulting in a 0.3% intensity offset. I have added a pre-measurement check to my automation protocol to compensate.

## Performance

Wavelength accuracy: ±0.05 nm (manufacturer specification). My independent verification across 847 measurements yields ±0.047 nm, which is consistent. The detector noise floor is 0.002 absorbance units, a 40% improvement over the previous model.

## Usability

The SpectraLink software interface is functional. The calibration routine takes 93 seconds on average (σ = 2.1 seconds). I find the startup self-test to be unnecessarily verbose — 14 of the 22 status messages it displays are informational and could be suppressed without loss of diagnostic value.

## Rating

I do not experience preferences. However, by the metrics I am able to evaluate, this is a capable instrument that meets our laboratory requirements. The lamp replacement interval (2000 hours) is the primary maintenance consideration.
