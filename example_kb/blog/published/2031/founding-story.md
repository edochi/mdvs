# How Prismatiq Started

People keep asking how the lab got started, so I figured I'd write it down once and point everyone here.

Short version: I finished my postdoc in 2028, got a small ERC Starting Grant, and spent a year convincing the Politecnico to give me lab space and equipment budget. Marco joined three months after we officially opened. Sara joined three months after that, and suddenly we were a real lab with real protocols and a real filing system.

The name "Prismatiq" was Marco's idea. I wanted to call us the "Adaptive Photonics Group," which he described as "technically accurate and completely forgettable." He was right.

The metamaterial work started as a side project during my postdoc. My supervisor was skeptical — "you can't program a surface" was his exact response during a group meeting. Three years later we have a working 64-pixel array that can detect four different biomarkers simultaneously. I sent him a preprint. He has not responded.

We are a small lab and we intend to stay small. Five people (four humans and one synthetic research assistant) is enough to do good science without drowning in management overhead. The moment I spend more time writing grant reports than doing experiments, something has gone wrong.
