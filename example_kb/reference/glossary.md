# Glossary

**Adaptive metamaterial** — An engineered surface whose optical properties can be reconfigured in real-time through electrical or optical stimulation.

**Biosensor array** — A chip containing multiple sensing elements (pixels), each capable of detecting a specific biological molecule.

**Calibration drift** — The gradual change in a sensor's response characteristics over time, requiring periodic recalibration.

**Metamaterial pixel** — A single tunable element on a biosensor array. Each pixel can be programmed to resonate at a specific wavelength.

**Resonance peak** — The wavelength at which a metamaterial pixel absorbs or reflects light most strongly. Shifts in the resonance peak indicate the presence of a target molecule.

**SPR** — Surface Plasmon Resonance. The optical phenomenon underlying our biosensor technology. When light hits a thin metal film at a specific angle, it generates surface plasmons whose behavior changes in the presence of bound molecules.

**Substrate** — The base material on which the metamaterial is fabricated. We use glass substrates coated with a gold-titanium bilayer.
